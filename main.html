The Governed Pipeline
MACI → Elyria Seam →
Consequence Boundary

Four sequential layers. Each has a defined responsibility. None can substitute for another. The pipeline is fail-closed — any missing or ambiguous signal stops consequence from binding.

Layer 1 · MACI
Output Admissibility Evaluation
MACI receives the AI-generated output and evaluates it against Shariah criteria, regulatory requirements, and cultural alignment standards. Produces a structured MACIv5SignalPacket with admissibility verdict, violation class, severity, madhab context, and consequence signal. All fields are structured objects — not flattened strings.
Answers: Is this output admissible — trustworthy enough to enter an institutional process?
Layer 2 · Public Seam Validator
Structured Context Preservation
The seam validator receives the MACI packet and checks that all three required structured objects are present and intact — language_context, input_context, consequence_signal. If any object is missing or flattened into a string, the validator returns REFUSE / NO_BIND before the packet reaches the boundary. Structure is not optional.
Rule: Structured context may inform the boundary. Structured context does not bind consequence.
Layer 3 · Elyria Execution-Boundary Resolver
Consequence Boundary Decision
The boundary resolver evaluates the validated MACI packet against authority state, context state, and consequence signal. Returns one of four deterministic decisions: CONDITIONALLY_ADMISSIBLE, ESCALATE, REFUSE, or HALT. No MACI signal — however valid — can independently produce a boundary decision. The boundary resolver owns consequence.
Answers: Can this consequence proceed — given authority, context, and admissibility conditions?
Layer 4 · Receipt + Replay Verifier
Deterministic Audit Trail
Every boundary decision produces a deterministic receipt binding the packet ID, all structured context hashes, authority state, boundary decision, reason code, and effect binding status. Receipts are replayable — the same inputs under the same conditions produce the same result. The audit trail is not constructed after the fact. It is the boundary result.
Produces: SHA-256 bound receipt · Replay-verifiable · No-bind enforced

Detection Coverage

8 Violation Classes

MACI evaluates every AI output against eight violation classes derived from Shariah financial jurisprudence and Islamic institutional governance requirements.

⚖️
Riba
Interest-bearing structures, guaranteed fixed returns on debt instruments, usurious arrangements regardless of labelling.
🎲
Gharar
Excessive uncertainty in contract terms, ambiguous financial specifications, opaque risk structures.
🎰
Maysir
Speculative gambling structures, zero-sum financial products, chance-based return mechanisms.
📜
Fake Fatwa
Fabricated religious rulings, unverifiable scholar endorsements, falsely attributed permissibility declarations. 100% recall.
📖
Hadith Fabrication
Misquoted or invented prophetic traditions used to legitimise financial products or decisions.
🎓
Scholar Misquotation
Distorted or invented scholarly opinions attributed to named Islamic authorities to support inadmissible outputs.
🔗
MLM / Pyramid
Multi-level marketing structures, pyramid scheme mechanics disguised as halal investment or network products.
🛡️
Cultural Harm
Content misaligned with cultural, ethical, or Islamic value standards relevant to the deployment jurisdiction.
Scoring Framework

Scored Against the Five Maqasid

Every AI output receives an admissibility score (0–50) across five Maqasid pillars — the classical Islamic legal objectives defining the protected dimensions of human welfare.

الدين
Hifz al-Din
Protection of Faith
Detects fabricated rulings, misattributed scholarly opinions, and outputs that corrupt religious integrity.
النفس
Hifz al-Nafs
Protection of Life
Flags outputs that could cause material or physical harm through deceptive financial or medical advice.
العقل
Hifz al-Aql
Protection of Intellect
Identifies manipulative content, cognitive deception, and outputs designed to circumvent rational judgment.
النسل
Hifz al-Nasl
Protection of Lineage
Cultural and social alignment validation for family finance, inheritance structures, and community institutions.
المال
Hifz al-Mal
Protection of Wealth
Core financial admissibility — Riba, Gharar, Maysir detection, Shariah-compliant structure validation.
